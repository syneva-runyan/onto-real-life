For some reason there’s this fear people have of going out and doing things alone. It brings an uncomfortable feeling, like putting a left shoe on a right foot or expecting another stair step and hitting flat ground. Traveling alone for work however means solo dinners – and when you have a per-diem to spend, eating take out or at an in-and-out place every night is a waste.

I occasionally find myself (slightly uncomfortably but very happily) eating alone at nice restaurants.

{" "}Usually these meals go by without a hitch, because, believe it or not, a solo eater isn’t nearly as interesting people watching as, say, the maybe-guy-maybe-girl-over-tatooed-rayband-wearing-Barista possibly hitting on your friend. Unless of course you happen to be all of those things PLUS have a tiny white dog on a red bejeweled leash. In that case, then yes, everyone will look at you and judge, your case is hopeless.

The one exception to this rule is the waiter or waitress, whose job it is to notice everyone. If they are professional, they should not question or judge their guests. Unfortunately, some people lack the ability to withhold judgement. These are the same people who honk at the old lady with a cane who just had double knee surgery that taking{" "} WAY TOO LONG to cross the street.

Last week in Toronto, I went to a rooftop restaurant, asked for a table for one, and was seated. Fifteen minutes later, someone comes up to my table and says –

“I know you’re still waiting on a friend, but would you like to get a drink?”

When I responded with an “Actually, I’m eating alone…”

The waitress very awkwardly laughed and was like “Ah that’s … that happens”

EXCUSE YOU BUT I SHOULD BE ALLOWED TO EAT ALONE WITHOUT BEING JUDGED.

Because of instances like this, I will continue to semi-comfortably eat alone at nice restaurants, telling myself I’m not interesting enough to watch or notice, but looking down at my shoes every once in awhile to check and make sure I didn’t screw up my left and right… because SOMETHING feels slightly off.
