There are plenty of practical things to spend money.

  • A house (no white picket fence though, never understood the appeal)
  • Unforgettable weekends binges (pinnacling at free shots for the entire bar and crash landing at 4am in a Waffle House)
  • A fanzy car the rapper-next-door gives a low whistle at every time you drive by

I’m confident that in another 10 years or so, all of these things will come to me (except the rapper next door, willing to admit that migghtttt be a stretch). And if they don’t? I’ll find equally practical things to fill those voids. For now though, my main spending outlet will be travel.

There are a million excuses that, if you’re not careful, will impede travel plans. I could list them, but everyone is familiar with the common themes. Money, time, worries of lonliness, etc. The value of travel experiences though surpass all concerns.

The first time I left the US, I was shocked at how similar people of different cultures were to me, even when they were drastically different. This fact is epitomised in a story I heard on{" "} Modern Love. A social worker in Philadelphia was tasked with counseling a group of Syrian refugee women. These women had stared into the face of starvation, had seen their cities and homes destroyed, had lost loved ones to war. The social worker worried. How could she begin to comprehend what these women had experienced and provide them help? When the women came to talk to her however, what did they have to say? They complained about their husbands being lazy, or the boy they who maybe-kinda-liked them.

Love – and love problems – are so universal. It is one of the many human emotions that traverses all cultural borders, that makes people relatable everywhere you go.

I want to understand the belief systems and lifestyles of people around the world. I want to see people who live differently than me, and know that it’s ok. I want to meet people living a completely different lives than me, and find aspects of their lives relatable.

I want to kill my prejudice, bigotry, and narrow-mindedness while I’m still young. The house, the binges, and the sweet ass car can wait.

Goal Number One: See the World.
