Rangitoto (Auckland)

Rangitoto is a volcanic island with an easy hike up and around the crater. There are lava caves worth exploring on the way up as well.

There's no open accomodation on the island, so would not recommend missing the last afternoon ferry.

Waiheke (Auckland)

Waiheke is one of the larger islands off the coast of Auckland. On Waiheke there are beautiful, expensive house with beautiful, expansive views. I went to Waiheke with the intention of drinking wine, which I did at various vinyards but I also managed to fit a nice hike in afterwards. One day wasn't enough time to see all of the island, and when I stop by on my way out of New Zealand, I'm excited to spend more time exploring here.

Breams Head (Whangarai)

Breams Head has been the most magestic hike of my trip so far.

At four hours, I chose the "short" trip.

Two hours of continous uphill are followed by windy bouldering to a breathtaking viewpoint, and then a muddy tramp back down the otherside of the mountain to an open bay.

Mount Parahak (Whangarai)

A 40 minute loop with spectacular panaromic views of Whangarai. Was accompanied by a random chicken at the summit.

Window Walk (Coromandel Peninsula)

Located in the Coromandel Peninsula, this was the most beautiful and interesting walk I've done.

It features an old mine with hiking trails built ontop of old mine cart tracks, which weave in and out of a mountain face (Bring a flashlight!). The "window walk" itself is only 20 minutes, but the area has a plethora of trails.

If you walk far enough and in the right direction you can make it to Owharoa Falls and Falls retreat, a cafe hidden in the woods with a warm heart, fairy lights, and delicious food.

Mount Victoria (Wellington)

Calling this a "hike" is a bit of a stretch. There is a car park at the top for easy access if you're feeling lazy.

Short but steep uphill climb that ends in beautiful 360deg views. Go during sunset or sunrise if you're brave
